# Net Worth Tracker — Outcomes

> Outcomes are the impact of what we build, not the things we build.
> Business outcomes measure the health of the business. Product outcomes are the leading indicators that predict them.

-----

## Business Outcomes

### BO1 — Reach 500 paying subscribers within year 1

The primary signal that a real gap exists in the market. Acquisition volume is partly a function of marketing effort, but the product has to convert and retain for the number to hold.

### BO2 — Achieve 60%+ trial-to-paid conversion

At €49/year, price is not the blocker. If a user doesn’t convert after their free month, the product didn’t deliver its promise fast enough. This measures whether the first experience creates a clear, compelling view of the user’s financial future.

### BO3 — 70%+ of paying subscribers start their second paid month

The signal that the product has ongoing value, not just novelty. A user who reaches month 3 (trial → paid month 1 → paid month 2) has found a place for this tool in their financial rhythm.

-----

## Product Outcomes

Product outcomes are leading indicators of the business outcomes above. They measure customer behavior in the product.

### PO1 — User reaches a meaningful projection in their first session

*Predicts: BO2 (trial-to-paid conversion)*

The quick-start wizard exists to make this happen. A user who sets up accounts, adds assumptions, and sees a net worth trajectory in session 1 is far more likely to convert.

Metric: % of trial users who complete a projection within 24 hours of signup.

### PO2 — User returns within 2 weeks of their first session

*Predicts: BO2 (trial-to-paid conversion)*

Habit formation starts early. A user who comes back unprompted in week 2 is signaling that the product earned a place in their rhythm — before they’ve been asked to pay.

Metric: % of trial users with a second session within 14 days.

### PO3 — User makes at least one planning action per month after onboarding

*Predicts: BO3 (month-1-to-month-2 renewal)*

This separates passive viewers from active planners. Someone who tweaks a scenario, updates an account balance, or runs a “what if” is getting ongoing value. A user who only ever looks is at churn risk.

Metric: % of paying subscribers who perform at least one planning action (scenario edit, account update, goal change) in a given month.

## Revenue Model

```
Annual Revenue = # of paying subscribers × €49/year
```

- Subscription: €49/year
- Trial: 1 month free, no commitment
- First retention gate: trial → paid (BO2)
- Second retention gate: paid month 1 → paid month 2 (BO3)

-----

## Focus

This is a market validation play. The question is: *does a meaningful gap exist in a crowded market?*

Paying customers answer that question. Retention answers whether the gap is real and durable.

**Current focus: BO2 and BO3** — conversion and early retention are the metrics most directly in the product team’s control and most telling about product-market fit.
